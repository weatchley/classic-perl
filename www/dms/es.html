This goal of understanding heat-driven coupled processes is not directly tied to a specific repository design and does not require the replication of potential repository conditions, geometries, and heating rates, nor is such replication practical in the DST. The fundamental nature of the DST is to test conceptual process models in the system and the ability to model quantitatively the systems response to those processes. Once confidence is developed in the coupled process models, they can be applied to any repository design. Such models provide a credible basis from which abstracted models can be developed for Total System Performance Assessment.

Based on the evolution of the design and knowledge gained to date from the heating phase, should the Project continue the DST as planned or modify the test to better address important hypotheses and uncertainties?

Recommendation:

Continue, as planned, with four years of heating to be followed by a similar cooling period. This includes the anticipated adjustments to the heater power in the near future to ensure smooth transition to the target drift-wall maximum temperature of 200C. In addition, evaluation of DST enhancements will continue. These include planning for the cooling phase in the near future, pending availability of resources, to allow for thorough consideration of all potential options to maximize the results of the test. Options to be considered would include those below and others deemed appropriate given the Repository Safety Strategy (RSS) and evolution of the design. Other enhancements will include periodic sidewall sampling in existing boreholes to gather mineralogic-petrologic data and conversion of a few chemistry boreholes into hydrology boreholes to obtain additional water samples.

Decision:

Accept recommendation.
